Green hydrogen is produced through the electrolysis of water, using renewable energy sources such as solar or wind power. The electrolysis process involves the splitting of water molecules into hydrogen and oxygen, using an electric current. There are several types of electrolysis technologies, including proton exchange membrane (PEM), alkaline, and solid oxide electrolysis. PEM electrolysis is the most commonly used technology, due to its high efficiency and ability to operate at high pressures.

The electrolysis process involves several steps, including water purification, electrolysis, and gas separation.
