**Technical Deep-Dive**

Green hydrogen is produced through the electrolysis of water, using renewable energy sources such as solar or wind power. The process involves the use of an electrolyzer, which splits water into hydrogen and oxygen. The hydrogen is then collected and stored for use in a variety of applications, including refining, ammonia, methanol, and iron & steel production.

There are several types of electrolyzers used in green hydrogen production, including alkaline electrolyzers, proton exchange membrane (PEM) electrolyzers, and solid oxide electrolyzers. Alkaline electrolyzers are the most common type of electrolyzer used in green hydrogen production, due to their low cost and high efficiency. PEM electrolyzers are also widely used, due to their high efficiency and ability to operate at high pressures.
