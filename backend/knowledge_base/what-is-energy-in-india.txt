**Energy Market Report: India 2024**

**Executive Summary**

India's energy market is undergoing significant transformations, driven by the country's growing energy demand and commitment to transition to renewable energy sources. Coal remains the dominant source of energy, but the government's goal to achieve 50% cumulative electric power installed capacity from non-fossil-based energy resources by 2030 is driving rapid growth in solar and other renewable energy. This report provides an analysis of the key trends, market opportunities, and risks in India's energy market, and offers insights into the future outlook for the sector.

**Key Trends**

1. **Coal dominance**: Coal remains the dominant source of energy in India, accounting for almost half of the total energy supply and 75% of electricity generation as of 2024.
2. **Rapid growth of renewable energy**: India is experiencing a rapid growth of solar and other renewable energy, with a goal to achieve 50% cumulative electric power installed capacity from non-fossil-based energy resources by 2030.
3. **Increasing reliance on fossil fuels**: Fossil fuels, including coal, oil, and natural gas, account for more than three-quarters of India's total energy supply.
4. **Growing energy demand**: India is the third-largest energy consumer globally, with a growing need to rapidly phase out coal and transition to renewable energy sources.
5. **Shift towards renewable energy**: India aims to achieve 45% non-fossil-fuel generation by 2030, with a focus on increasing renewable energy capacity, including solar, wind, and hydroelectric power.

**Market Impact**

The rapid growth of renewable energy in India presents significant market opportunities, including:

1. **Renewable energy investments**: The rapid growth of renewable energy in India presents opportunities for investments in solar, wind, and hydroelectric power.
2. **Energy storage solutions**: As India transitions to renewable energy, there is a growing need for energy storage solutions to ensure a stable and reliable energy supply.
3. **Green infrastructure development**: The growth of renewable energy in India presents opportunities for the development of green infrastructure, including solar parks, wind farms, and hydroelectric power plants.
4. **Energy efficiency services**: The increasing focus on renewable energy and energy efficiency presents opportunities for energy efficiency services, including energy audits and energy management systems.

However, the rapid phase-out of coal and transition to renewable energy also presents significant risks, including:

1. **Coal phase-out challenges**: The rapid phase-out of coal presents challenges for the Indian energy sector, including the need for significant investments in renewable energy infrastructure and the potential for job losses in the coal industry.
2. **Energy security risks**: The growing reliance on renewable energy sources presents energy security risks, including the potential for power outages and grid instability.
3. **Fossil fuel dependence**: India's continued dependence on fossil fuels presents risks, including the potential for price volatility and the impact of climate change.
4. **Infrastructure development risks**: The growth of renewable energy in India presents infrastructure development risks, including the need for significant investments in transmission and distribution infrastructure.

**Future Outlook**

India's energy market is expected to continue to undergo significant transformations in the coming years, driven by the government's commitment to transition to renewable energy sources. The country is expected to achieve its goal of 50% cumulative electric power installed capacity from non-fossil-based energy resources by 2030, and to reduce its dependence on fossil fuels. However, the rapid phase-out of coal and transition to renewable energy also presents significant challenges, including the need for significant investments in renewable energy infrastructure and the potential for job losses in the coal industry.
